Locating

The Dungeness Ruins is located on Cumberland Island off St. Marys, Georgia. The island is only accessible during the day by private boat or the St. Marys ferry.

History

Cumberland Island had undergone several primary owners first recorded in 1765. The island was primary used as residence for British and American war efforts. The final owners were the Carnegie family who purchased a large portion of the island in the 1880s. The Dungeness mansion was built along with the notable Plum Orchard mansion and the Greyfield which remains as the only usable building on the island as a hotel. The Dungeness was burned down in 1959 with current suspicions of arson.

Horses are foreign to the island with several breeds being introduced by the Spanish, British, and the last owners, the Carnegies, from the 16th to the 19th century. It is suggested that the Spanish horses could not have survived until the British arrived and are not related to the currently living horses. Horses can be found throughout the island and primarily nearby the Duck Pond and Dungeness ruins. Horse populations are estimated around 150 - 200 horses and their lifespans are significantly shorter due to the harsh habitat. Several statements and efforts have been made to address the poor living conditions and the negative impact horses have on the environment. No management plan is in place as of 2026.

As of 1972, the National Park Service maintains and owns the Dungeness ruins and a large amount of the island. Some parts of Cumberland Island is still owned privately.

Important Notes

  • Horses attacks have occured. Always maintain a safe distance and do not feed any horses. If a horse changes behavior due to your presence, create a longer distance.
  • Horse droppings are scattered across the island, and are heavily found around the Dungeness Ruins. Bring additional shoes if you prefer.
  • Unshaded pathways, most notably the path to the southern beach due to the sand, can be difficult to manage even at 80° F. Avoid trips around the summer time.
  • If you want to stay past 5pm for any reason or before 9am, you will be required to camp overnight or reside at the Greyfield Inn.

Warnings & Tips

Warnings

The horses are feral animals and should be treated as such.

Travel Advisory

Water fountains are seldom found throughout the island. Bring a bottle to refill.

Expected Stay

Approximately 4 hours (scheduled ferry departures).

Note: If you miss the last ferry departure, you are stranded on the island overnight.

Accessibility

Ferry-accessible only. Sand and dirt pathways only. Off-road wheelchairs are available by reservation.
