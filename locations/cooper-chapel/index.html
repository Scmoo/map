Locating

The Cooper Chapel is accessible from dedicated parking off Arkansas Highway 340. Cooper Chapel stands tall in the midst of forest trees with a short stone pathway leadings to the front doors.

Photos

History

The Cooper Chapel was built in honor of Mildred B. Cooper by her family in 1988. Mildred Cooper was very active in her family's life, church, school, and the Arkansas community. The Cooper Chapel is designed to provide a source of inspiration to those who visit, similar to Mildred Cooper's inspiration during her life.

The chapel has been regarded as one of Arkansas's most beautiful locations, making it a popular option for wedding cermonies. The first event at the chapel was a wedding in February 1988.

Important Notes

  • Throughout most days the Cooper Chapel remains unoccupied, making it an excellent visit for those wanting a quiet retreat.
  • There are nearby stores and restaurants within a 15 minute drive for refreshments.
  • Designers E. Fay Jones and Maurice Jennings together have numerous other prestigious chapels. Included is Thorncrow Chapel nearby at Eureka Springs and Garvan Gardens Carillon at Hot Springs.
  • Normally open to the public during opening times, Cooper Chapel is seldomly reserved for private occasions.
